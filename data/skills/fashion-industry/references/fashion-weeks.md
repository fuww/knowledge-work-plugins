# Fashion Week Calendar

Comprehensive schedule of major fashion weeks worldwide. Fashion weeks showcase ready-to-wear collections approximately 6 months before retail availability (SS collections shown in September, AW shown in February).

## Big Four Fashion Weeks

The "Big Four" drive global fashion coverage and set seasonal trends.

### New York Fashion Week (NYFW)

| Season | Typical Dates | Location | Focus |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------|
| AW (February) | Early-mid February | Spring Studios, various venues | American ready-to-wear |
| SS (September) | Early-mid September | Spring Studios, various venues | American ready-to-wear |

**Key characteristics:**
- Commercial focus, buyer-friendly schedules
- Strong in sportswear, American sportswear heritage
- Emerging designer platforms (NYFW: The Shows)
- Celebrity front rows, media coverage

**Major presentations:** Marc Jacobs, Michael Kors, Ralph Lauren, Tommy Hilfiger, Coach, Carolina Herrera, Proenza Schouler, Tory Burch

---

### London Fashion Week (LFW)

| Season | Typical Dates | Location | Focus |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------|
| AW (February) | Mid-late February | 180 The Strand, BFC Show Space | British design, emerging talent |
| SS (September) | Mid-late September | 180 The Strand, BFC Show Space | British design, sustainability |

**Key characteristics:**
- Strong on creativity and emerging talent
- British Fashion Council (BFC) organized
- NEWGEN sponsorship for new designers
- Sustainability initiatives (Fashion Positive)
- Accessible to emerging designers

**Major presentations:** Burberry, JW Anderson, Simone Rocha, Erdem, Victoria Beckham, Christopher Kane, Molly Goddard, Richard Quinn

### Milan Fashion Week (MFW)

| Season | Typical Dates | Location | Focus |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------|
| AW (February) | Late February - early March | Various venues across Milan | Italian luxury, craftsmanship |
| SS (September) | Mid-late September | Various venues across Milan | Italian luxury, accessories |

**Key characteristics:**

### Paris Fashion Week (PFW)

| Season | Typical Dates | Location | Focus |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------|
| AW (February/March) | Late February - early March | Various venues across Paris | French couture heritage, international luxury |
| SS (September/October) | Late September - early October | Various venues across Paris | French design, global luxury |

**Key characteristics:**
- Closes the fashion month circuit
- Federation de la Haute Couture et de la Mode organized
- Mix of French heritage houses and international brands
- Major finale shows (Chanel, Louis Vuitton)
- Showrooms and off-schedule events

**Major presentations:** Chanel, Louis Vuitton, Dior, Saint Laurent, Balenciaga, Givenchy, Valentino, Celine, Loewe, Hermes, Alexander McQueen

---

## Paris Haute Couture

Separate from ready-to-wear, haute couture shows custom-made luxury.

| Season | Typical Dates | Focus |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Spring/Summer | Late January | Custom-made for following summer |
| Autumn/Winter | Early July | Custom-made for following winter |

**Key characteristics:**
- "Haute couture" is a protected term in France
- Members must apply to Federation de la Haute Couture
- Made-to-measure, hand-crafted pieces
- Limited number of official members (~15 active)
- Guest members and correspondents also show

**Official haute couture members:** Chanel, Dior, Givenchy, Jean Paul Gaultier, Schiaparelli, Valentino, Elie Saab, Zuhair Murad, Alexis Mabille

## Fashion Week Coverage Planning

### Pre-Event (2-4 weeks before)
- Schedule preview articles
- Designer profile features
- Trend prediction pieces
- What to expect guides

### During Event (each day)
- Daily runway recaps
- Street style galleries
- Designer interviews
- Real-time social coverage
- Trend spotting

### Post-Event (1-2 weeks after)
- Trend analysis reports
- Best collections roundup
- Business analysis
- Buyer reaction pieces
- Retail implications
